01
Fintech GCC Market $250K–$307K

MashaPay — Multi-Chain Crypto Payment Platform

Founder & Project Manager Kammy Agency
The Problem

A well-funded GCC startup needed a payment platform that let users pay merchants in crypto while merchants received fiat — instantly, across seven blockchains, with full compliance. The technical complexity was enormous, and the client needed a clear, phased plan to secure investor confidence.

What I Did

I developed a comprehensive milestone-based proposal valued at $250K–$307K. I mapped the full system architecture — integrating Fireblocks for custody, Binance Convert API for liquidity, and a double-entry ledger system ensuring auditability across seven chains. I then broke the project into 6 clear milestones with deliverables, timelines, and resource plans, and presented directly to founders.

Real Estate Blockchain Audit 6 Months Saved

Real Estate Tokenization MVP Audit & Rescue

Technical Auditor Project Strategy Lead
The Problem

The client had invested in an MVP built on Laravel and MySQL to tokenize property assets. It was rigid, couldn't scale to handle complex smart contract interactions, and was architecturally misaligned with their growth plans. They needed an honest, actionable technical review before going further.

What I Did

I conducted a full system audit: reviewing code structure, database design, and smart contract integration points. I wrote a detailed report highlighting where the existing stack would fail under real load, and then proposed a complete rebuild strategy using Node.js and MongoDB — far better suited for high-frequency blockchain transactions.

Results

The founders adopted the rebuild plan. They avoided what would have been a costly, dead-end development path, and the new architecture is currently being implemented. My audit saved them an estimated six months of wasted effort and significant capital in rework costs.

Excelerate PM Internship Program Design

Project Lead PM Intern · Dubai | USA
The Problem

Excelerate needed two structured, hands-on internship programs that would actually simulate real-world project management — not just theory. They lacked a repeatable template that interns and mentors could follow across cohorts. Programs had to be self-contained, deliverable-driven, and runnable within 4 weeks.

What I Did

I led the end-to-end design of both programs from scratch — writing Team and Project Charters, structuring two distinct 4-week internship briefs with weekly deliverables, setting up Trello boards to mirror real Agile/Kanban workflows, and applying Scrum principles where each week was a sprint and each deliverable a working increment.

Manager, Project Management Kami Empowerment Cooperative
The Problem

What I Did

I implemented full PMBOK-aligned project systems using only free tools: Google Workspace for charters, risk registers, communication plans, and KPI tracking.
